Working paper

Perceived Income Risks (Job Marekt Paper)[Preliminary Draft][Bib]

  • Stylized facts on perceived income risks revealed in density surveys in comparison to the estimated income risks used in incomplete market macro models.
  • An incomplete-market OLG-GE model with heterogeneous income risks calibrated from the survey data.
  • Heterogeneity in income risks as a new observable factor accounting for wealth inequality.
  • Lower perceived risks in the survey than conventional estimates helps account for low-liquid-asset-holdings of hands-to-mouth agents.

Learning from Friends in a Pandemic: Social Networks and the Macroeconomic Response of Consumption[Working Paper][Slides][GitHub][SSRN][VoxEU][Bib]

(with Christos Makridis)
  • Evidence for social network as a potential propagation mechanism of aggregate consumption response to the pandemic.
  • Counties more socially connected to regions hardly hit by the pandemic saw bigger drop in consumption.
  • Consumption categories relied upon social contact saw the biggest responses.
  • A network-augmented heterogenous-agent consumption model showing how idiosyncratic shocks can be translated into aggregate responses depending on the social network structure and location of initial shocks.

How Do Agents Form Inflation Expectations? Evidence from the Forecast Uncertainty[Working Paper][GitHub][Bib]

  • Cross-moment estimates of various workhorse theories of expectation formation.
  • Use of high moments such as forecast uncertainty available from density forecast.
  • Expectations data contains useful information about inflation dynamics itself.
